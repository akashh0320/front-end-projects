Welcome to biomedical Engineering

Biomedical Engineering combines engineering principles with medical and biological science to improve healthcare systems and patient care.

Topics in Biomedical Engineering

Medical devices

Explore the design and innovation of devices used in healthcare, from pacemakers to diagnostic tools.

Biomaterials

Study of materials used in medical implants, prosthetics, and drug delivery systems.

Biomechanics

Research into growing tissues and organs for medical applications and regenerative therapies.

Rehabilitation Engineering

Rehabilitation engineering focuses on designing and developing technologies to improve the quality of life and functional abilities of individuals with disabilities.

Advances in Biomedical Engineering

3D BioPrinting in Healthcare

This technology allows the creation of complex biological structures, such as tissues and organs, using biocompatible materials and cells. It holds potential for organ transplantation and regenerative medicine.

Neuroprosthetics and Brain-Machine Interfaces

Devices such as advanced prosthetics and brain-machine interfaces enable individuals with disabilities to control devices using neural signals.
